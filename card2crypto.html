Questions

We've all the answers.

Rather have the conversation directly? Email daniel@southpay.io. A human replies.

What verticals do you actually serve? +
Peptides and research chemicals, CBD/hemp/kratom, and adult AI (companion apps, generative adult content platforms, adult SaaS). If you're borderline or in another restricted category, email us. We take the conversation before we say no.
How is this different from Commerce? +
Commerce is our self-serve crypto checkout at 1.99% flat. Customers pay in crypto, you settle in USD or stablecoin. Card2Crypto keeps the customer experience as a normal card, Google Pay, Apple Pay or ACH payment. We handle the acceptance on a rail built for your vertical, and you settle non-custodially in stablecoin. Different product, different rail, different risk model.
Why doesn't this page show a price? +
Because the number depends on your vertical, monthly volume, average ticket, refund rate, and where funds land. A one-size-fits-all rate would either overcharge low-risk merchants or bankrupt us on the higher-risk ones. We quote in the intro call.
Are there monthly fees or setup fees? +
No monthly subscription. No setup fee. Per-transaction pricing only, quoted per merchant.
Do you hold rolling reserves? +
No standing reserve as a default. For very high refund or chargeback verticals we may agree a short-term reserve during onboarding, but the "hold 15% for six months just because" model does not apply here.
Where do funds settle? +
Directly in stablecoin (USDC or USDT) to a wallet you control. Settlement is non-custodial, so funds are yours from the moment they land and you can withdraw immediately. No US bank required and no third party holding your money.
Are chargebacks really impossible? +
On the crypto leg of settlement to you, yes. It's final and irreversible. On the acceptance leg from the customer's card, standard card-network dispute rules apply upstream on our rail. We absorb that risk as part of the service, not you.
How long does onboarding take? +
Usually inside two weeks from first email to first processed charge. Underwriting review, contract, integration walk-through, then live.
